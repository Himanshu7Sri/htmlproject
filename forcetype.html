Force And Momentum

Force In the second law of newton , newton gave the definition of force

Acceleration - Rate of change of velocity is known as acceleration .

Retardation - Negative acceleration ( acceleration is happening but in opposite direction ) is known as retardation . it is also known as deceleration .

Newton's Second Law - the vector sum of the forces F on an object is equal to the mass m ( it is suppose that mass of that object is constant ) of that object multiplied by the acceleration a of the object: F = ma .



Momentum - Product of mass and velocity is known as momentum .
Force can also be defined or calculated as rate of change of momentum is known as Force .
F = dP/dt = change in momentum / Time



during change in momentum there can be two possibility -
1. Mass of system remains constant
2. Mass of the system varies

In most of cases mass of the system does not varie so we can write the equation as -
F = mdv/dt

if mass and velocity of the system varie then we can use a formula -
F = change in(mv)/time


Now consider a scenario in which a body is resting on a table . what prevent the body from falling down
Here , The table exerts a normal force on the body . this is called action force and in return of that the body exerts a downward normal force on the table this is called reaction force . the action and the reaction force act on the body and table .

Newton's Third Law - For every action there is equal and opposite reaction .
