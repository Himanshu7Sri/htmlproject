Rationalisation Factor - It is also known as Rationalisation of Denominator . as here come some expression containing square-root in their denominator so to make denominator free from square-roots we just multiply the numerator and denominator by an irrational number and that irrational number may be -

if the denominator are of the form (a + √b) than mulltiply numerator and denominator by ( a - √b ) similarly .
if the denominator are of the form (a - √b) than mulltiply numerator and denominator by ( a + √b ) .
Lets see some example based on the Rationalisation Factor -


Example 1. Rationalise the denominator of 8 / √5 - √7 .
Solution -
   multiply √5 + √7 to numerator and denominator
   = 8(√5 + √7) / (√5 - √7)(√5 + √7)
   = 8(√5 + √7) / (√52 - √72)
   = 8(√5 + √7) / (5 - 7)
   = 8(√5 + √7) / (-2)
   = -4(√5 + √7)

Example 2. Rationalise the denominator of 1 / 5 + 3√2 .
Solution -
   multiply 5 - 3√2 to numerator and denominator
   = 1(5 - 3√2) / (5 + 3√2)(5 - 3√2)
   = (5 - 3√2) / (52 - (3√2)2)
   = (5 - 3√2) / (25 - (9 X 2))
   = (5 - 3√2) / (25 - 18)
   = (5 - 3√2) / 7

Example 3. Rationalise the denominator of √3 + 1 / √3 - 1 .
Solution -
   multiply (√3 + 1) to numerator and denominator
   = (√3 + 1)(√3 + 1) / (√3 - 1)(√3 + 1)
   = (√3 + 1)2 / (√32 - 12)
   = (3 + 1 + 2√3) / (9 - 1)
   = (4 + 2√3) / 8
   = (2 + √3) / 4
