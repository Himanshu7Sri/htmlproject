Introduction

Natural Number -> Counting numbers are known as natural number .
Example - 1 , 2 , 3 , . . . . . . . , ∞

Whole Number -> counting number along with zero are known as whole number .
Example - 0 , 1 , 2 , 3 , . . . . . . , ∞

Integer -> Natural number along with 0 and negative natural number are known as integer .
Example - -∞ , . . . . . , -2 , -1 , 0 , 1 , 2 , . . . . . . , ∞
Note - Every whole number and natural number is an integer but vice versa is not true .

Rational Number -> All those numbers are rational number which can be written in the form of p/q , where q ≠ 0 .
Example - 0 is rational number as 0/1 .
Note - Every whole number , natural number and integers are rational number .

Equivalent Rational Number -> Rational number after solving the values become same .
Example - 15/30 = 1/2 & 144/288 = 1/2 both are equivalent rational number because after solving both values are 1/2 .


Real Number -> any number what you can think and that does not contains iota ( i ) are known as real number .
Example - 0 , -1 , √2 , √3 , 0.66666.. , 0.101101110... , 5/7 . . . . .

Note => N ⊂ W ⊂ Z ⊂ Q ⊂ R
N - Natural Number
W - Whole Number
Z - Integer
Q - Rational Number
R - Real Number
