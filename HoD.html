Height and Distance

Example 1. An observer 1.5 m tall is 28.5 m away from a chimney . the angle of elevation of the top of the chimney from the eyes is 45° . what is the height of the chimney ?
Solution : let height of the chimney is x
distance between chimney and the boy is ( d ) 28.5 m
angle of elevation is 45°
height of the boy is 1.5

tan 45° = ( x - 1.5 ) / d
1 = ( x - 1.5 ) / 28.5
x - 1.5 = 28.5
x = 28.5 + 1.5
x = 30 m


Example 1. From a point on a bridge across the river , the angle of depression of the bank of opposite sides of the river are 30° and 45° , respectively . if the bridge is at the height of 3 m from the bank , find the width of the river .
Solution : let width of the river is x
height ( h ) 3 m
angle of depression is 30° and 45°

tan 30° = h / AD
1 / √3 = 3 / AD
AD = 3√3 m
now , for calculating the next half width
tan 45° = h / BD
1 = 3 / BD
BD = 3 m

Total width of the river is x = AD + BD
x = 3√3 m + 3 m
