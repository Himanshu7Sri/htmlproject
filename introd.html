Introduction to Heron's Formula

Heron was born in about 10AD possibly in Alexandria in Ezypt. He worked in applied mathematics . His work on mathematical and physical subject are so numerous and varied that he is considered to be an encyclopedic writer in these field . his geometrical works deal largely with problems on mensuration written three books. Book 1 deals with the area of squares , rectangle , triangle , various other specialised quadrilaterals , the regular polygons , circle , surface area of cone , cylinders , spheres etc .

If we want to calculate the area of right angled triangle so we'll easily calculate by using formula

Area of Triangle = 1/2 x base x height

Here we will discuss about the famous formula which Heron has derived for the area of a triangle in terms of its three sides .

Area of Triangle = √( s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c) )

where a , b , c are the sides of triangle and s = semi-parameter i.e. half of the parimeter of the triangle
s = ( a + b + c ) / 2 ;
