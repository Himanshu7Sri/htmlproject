Remark - Frictional force doesn't depend upon the area of body of contact , but depend on how hard the two bodies are pressing against each other and the type of surface . it act opposite to the direction of motion of a body .

Coefficient of friction - A coefficient of friction is a value that shows the relationship between the force of friction between two objects and the normal reaction between the objects that are involved . it can be represented as μ .

f = μN


N is the normal force .

Example - A block is moving with some velocity . if mass of the block is 5 kg and coefficient of friction is 0.4 , find out the frictional force acting on the body (take g = 10).

Solution - f = μN
f = μ x mg
f = 0.4 x 5 x 10
f = 20 N

Types of Frictional force -
1. Static friction
2. Sliding friction
3. Kinetic friction

Static Friction - the friction that exists between a stationary object and the surface on which it's resting. A frictional force occurs when you try to push an object alongside a surface .
fs = μsN



Kinetic Friction - It is a force that acts between moving surfaces. An object that is being moved over a surface will experience a force in the opposite direction as its movement. The magnitude of the force depends on the coefficient of kinetic friction between the two kinds of material .
